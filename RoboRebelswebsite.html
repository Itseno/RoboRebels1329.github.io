Description:

Our robotics club competes in the worldwide FIRST Robotics Competition. We focus on mechanical, electrical
and software engineering as well as team management (business, public relations, fundraising, safety).

Benefits:

The opportunity to explore many aspects of STEM (science, technology, engineering and mathematics)
in a supportive environment where failure is a learning experience;

  1. Hone creative, business and PR skills.

  2. Looks great on your transcripts.

  3. Opportunities for scholarships.

  4. Meeting pioneering companies at competitions.

When and Where:

  • Preseason (beginning of school - December 31): We will have various meetings during preseason,
    which are announced towards the beginning of the school year.

  • Season (first Saturday of January - mid-March): Meet for at least four hours (2 x 2 hour work session)
    per week to work on the robot. Signups for these are towards the end of the year. Flexibility is key;
    we understand our students' tough schedules. Sessions are M/W/F (5-7, 7-9), Saturday (12-2, 2-4), and
    Sunday (2-4, 4-6). If we make it to the championships, our schedule is extended to late April.

  • Post-Season (TBD): After the season we have a final team meeting and party. Date depends
    on championship participation.

  • Team meetings are held in Room 4, 5, 6, and 8 of the Junior School. Work sessions are in the Robotics
    room and lower commons of the Junior School.

Prerequisites:

You must be a hard worker and willing to learn.
